# About me

I am a Control and Automation Engineer with a passion for mobile robotics, computer vision, machine learning, and edge computing. I earned both my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from the [Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC)](https://en.ufsc.br/), one of the most prestigious engineering schools in South America. I have also spent one year as a visiting student at [Brown University](https://www.brown.edu/), where I worked as a research assistant in the [Laboratory for Engineering Man/Machine Systems (LEMS)](http://vision2.lems.brown.edu/) under supervision of [Prof. Benjamin Kimia](https://vivo.brown.edu/display/bkimia#).
<br/><br/>
In my Master’s thesis, I worked with [Prof. Alexandre Trofino](http://trofino.das.ufsc.br/en/) on state estimation strategies for airborne wind energy systems. My work was primarily focused on the real-time localization of the aircraft and in-flight identification of its aerodynamic characteristics, and led to publications in prominent venues such as the IEEE TCST, IFAC, AWEC, REDEC, as well as international collaborations with research groups from the Politecnico di Milano and the Grenoble Institute of Technology.
<br/><br/>
I grew up in [Ijuí](https://goo.gl/maps/X5WMck7KNqHFnajb8), a small city in the south of Brazil, but have lived in Porto Alegre, Florianópolis, Providence, Berlin and, more recently, in Luxembourg. In my spare time, I enjoy reading, playing footbal, biking, and programming.
