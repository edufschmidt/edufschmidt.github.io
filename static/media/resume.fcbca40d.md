* **Airborne Wind Energy Laboratory, UFSC**\
*Master's Student*<span style="display:inline;float:right;text-align:right;font-weight:bold;">Mar 2016 - Sep 2017</span>\
Investigated estimation strategies for the real-time localization and aerodynamics identification of tethered aircraft. Developed an EKF-based filtering structure capable of delivering reliable estimates of several variables of interest, having demonstrated its effectiveness both in a simulation environment and on a small-scale prototype. Led the development of the embedded hardware and software of a 12 kW Airborne Wind Energy system, and supervised a small team of students working on related topics.

* **Department of Automation and Systems, UFSC**\
*Research Assistant*<span style="display:inline;float:right;text-align:right;font-weight:bold;">Oct 2013 - Jul 2014</span>\
Worked under supervision of Prof. Alexandre Trofino on the development of a machine learning pipeline for multiclass motor imagery classification from raw EEG data. Studied and implemented several artifact removal, feature extraction, dimensionality reduction, and classification algorithms including CSP, LDA,PCA, ICA, SVM, and logistic regression.

* **Laboratory for Man-Machine Systems, Brown University**\
*Research Assistant*<span style="display:inline;float:right;text-align:right;font-weight:bold;">Jun 2013 - Mar 2014</span>\
Worked under supervision of Prof. Benjamin Kimia on the development of an assisted navigation system for the visually impaired involving computer vision, inertial sensors, and collaborative mapping. Responsibilities included the design of the overall software architecture of the system, the construction and calibration of a basic stereo rig, and the implementation of a visual odometry algorithm using C++, OpenCV, and Eigen.

* **Hydraulic and Pneumatic Systems Laboratory, UFSC**\
*Research Assistant*<span style="display:inline;float:right;text-align:right;font-weight:bold;">Mar 2012 - Aug 2012</span>\
Worked on the design, implementation, and evaluation of different control strategies for a hydraulically-driven robotic manipulator. The techniques employed ranged from simple PID controllers with adaptive gains to more complex structures based on the inverse dynamics of the system with compensation for important nonlinearities such as dead-zone and friction.
